ABOUT MUSEUM OF SCIENCE AND TECHNOLOGY

The Museum of Science and Technology in Accra was established when two lecturers from the Legon Campus of the University of Ghana, identified a need to create awareness of past and current developments in science and technology in Ghana, and presented a proposal for the development of the institution to Kwame Nkrumah, the then president of Ghana. A short time later, in 1965, the Museum of Science and Technology opened its doors. The institution endeavors to inspire those who visit, particularly its more youthful guests, with a desire to discover more about the sciences and technological development in the world around them.
The Museums of Science and Technology is set up in Accra in building on the same premises as the National Museum. The Museum of Science and Technology is mandated to collect, conserve, maintain and preserve the collections of scientific value, and to document the past inventions and the technological achievements and present them side by side with the modern scientific and technological developments for the educational purpose and the enjoyment of society. The museum ensures the scientific skills and appropriate technologies are effectively demonstrated and also transferred to the general public at large for the benefit of both the small and the large scale industries, and to support science education and technology at all levels.
